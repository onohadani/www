Omid Nohadani leads the Data Science team at Benefits Science, developing optimization and machine learning solutions for managing and structuring risk in health plans. He is a professor at Northwestern University (and previously at Purdue), focusing on data-driven algorithms. He received his Ph.D. in physics from the University of Southern California. Dr. Nohadani was a postdoctoral researcher at the Operations Research Center at MIT and a research fellow at Harvard Medical School. He received several research awards, including the best paper award for IISE Transactions on Healthcare Systems Engineering (2018) and the best regional paper award of the American Association for Physicists in Medicine (2013).

Nohadani's research is at the intersection of optimization and machine learning to control risk in complex systems under uncertainty, with applications in healthcare, supply chain management, analytics, and technology. In particular, he studies dynamic and data-driven systems with decision makers who face complex risk requirements. These integrated decision environments often require computationally challenging solutions. Methodologically, his research is on robust optimization which is capable of improving performance by accounting for uncertainties. His methodological work is motivated by uncovering parallels between different applications and bridging seemingly disjoint disciplines, with a proven record of leveraging these abstract approaches for solving real-world problems. A number of his results are already implemented in medical and industrial applications.
